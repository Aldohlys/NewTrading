### Semiconductors — full parabolic, KOSPI bubble warning
"Full parabolic mode on the semiconductors." Yesterday's earnings: **AMD ripped \$350 → \$420**, dragging SMH ripping higher. **NVDA**: "getting a little bullish tailwind, but not the winner" in this leg. The real winner is **KOSPI**, which has gone full parabolic — Patrick calls it "the most ridiculous bubble out there" and analogizes it to **Canadian markets in the dot-com bubble** when Nortel was going stupid: two stocks now represent ~50% of the entire Korean index, dictating the move.

- **KOSPI measured move**: **\$7,500**, but already past white-heat parabolic. Doesn't know where the price top is — that's the unpredictable part. *Time* is far more predictable: a swing high typically lands within 1-2 weeks of going parabolic.
- **Don't short white-heat.** "Stupid can get much stupider very quickly." The real short on bubbles is **the second rally after the first blow-off**, not the peak. Analog: gold's right-side short was *not* at the parabolic top but later, after the first blow-off and a retracement rally. For KOSPI, expect: \$8,000+ first, then a "stupid drop," then a fib retrace, and **June/July** is when bubble shorts kick in.
- **AMD weekly chart**: ~100% in 4-5 weeks since the move started. Blow-off behavior. At some point, these will revert — but timing is on the *other side of the mountain*.

### Spread management & hedge-roll Q&A
- **SMH May bull call spreads**: hold for the full win. Don't kill or roll early. A spread is nowhere near max profit until both legs are in the money at expiration. If semis tack on \$30-40 more, you want both legs ITM. "Strap on your seatbelt — semis could literally go in the money, run to 600, and be back under 500 all within a week."
- **Put-spread hedge roll-up to higher strikes** (general): **No.** Even if SPX peaks at 7,500, expect "several months of the market doing nothing" — rolling up puts adds capital and accelerates theta on a position not immediately needed. **Leave tail hedges as-is.** When spidey senses tingle, "we slam our hedges as high as we can and lock in as much as we can." Not now.
