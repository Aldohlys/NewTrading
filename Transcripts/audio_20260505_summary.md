### Trade of the week — NVDA (long stock + protective put, overlapping earnings)
The whole tutorial is about exploiting *rising* vol-premium pre-earnings as a positive carry rather than getting "plowed over by it."

- **Thesis:** semis are leadership; if the trend holds, NVDA retests prior highs in the **\$215-220** range over the next 2 weeks. **Not** an earnings bet — the position will be punted if NVDA hasn't risen by the **May 19** breakout session (day before May 20 earnings).
- **Vol-crush primer using PLTR** as the counterexample: into Palantir earnings, the front-tenor IV climbed **70% → 80% → 90% → 100% → ~110%** in the final week. *After* earnings, IV imploded. That's the normal reaction function — and exactly what Patrick wants to *avoid* paying.
- **The asymmetry:** the option that *overlaps* NVDA earnings is being priced at **48% IV** vs. **~40%** on other-tenor options that don't touch the earnings event. The ATM \$200 put is **~\$5** in the non-earnings tenor vs **~\$8.50** in the May 22 earnings-overlapping tenor — a ~\$3.50 vol-premium difference (~10-12 vol points).
- **Why pay the premium intentionally:** as you walk *toward* earnings, IV on the May 22 tenor will rise (analog: PLTR's 70→110 IV ramp). The rising **Vega** on that option *offsets the theta*, so the hedge carries cheaper than it looks. By contrast, the May 15 non-earnings put bleeds full theta if the stock doesn't move — if nothing happens, the **May 15 put loses ~\$500** (full premium) while the **May 22 put** at static vol loses only ~\$293, and at rising IV (49% → ~75-80% pre-earnings) carries close to zero.
- **Structure:** buy 100 shares NVDA + buy 1 May 22 \$200 put. Risk capped ~**\$800**.
- **Synthetic alternative:** June expiration deep-ITM 80-delta call (~**\$180 strike, ~\$23**) — same rule applies (punt before earnings if stock hasn't moved). Spreads are very tight, NVDA highly liquid.
- **Hail-Mary alternative:** May 22 **\$215 call ~\$2.80** OTM. As earnings approaches, that 215 call's IV climbs to 70-80%; very little theta burn; biggest Vega kick for an OTM call. Acceptable Hail Mary that uses the vol-ramp as a tailwind.
- **Exit rule:** if NVDA is still at the **50DMA / \$195-200** going into May 19, punt. Don't eat the Vega collapse on the other side. If NVDA is at \$210-215 by May 19, then earnings gamble is on the table.
