## Technical View

### What Broke
- 50-day moving average likely violated (9% in 8 days cuts through it)
- Uptrend channel from mid-2024 broken (channel slope ~5-7%/month)
- Drop went through Oct 2025 congestion zone (~$396) which should have been support

### What Recovery Needs (Technical)
1. **Hold $390-400 zone** — Oct 2025 breakout level. Below $390 → next support $350-360 (mid-2025). That confirms trend change, not correction.
2. **Reclaim 50MA from below** — first rally back to 50MA often gets sold. Must reclaim and hold, not just touch.
3. **Volume confirmation** — selloff needs to exhaust. Look for high-volume capitulation then declining volume on further dips.
4. **Time** — a 20-month trend that breaks doesn't typically V-bottom. Expect base-building over weeks, not days.

### IV Context
IVP was already 84 at the $445 top — elevated fear priced in. IV30 likely spiked further at $404. Options expensive for re-entry.

## Macro View

### Rally Drivers (2024-2026)
1. Central bank buying (China, India, Turkey, Poland) — de-dollarization
2. Rate cut expectations — lower real rates = higher gold
3. Geopolitical risk premium — Middle East, Ukraine, trade wars
4. Fiscal concerns — US deficit trajectory

### Possible Reversal Catalysts
- **Tariff/trade war escalation** — counterintuitively gold-negative if forced liquidation across asset classes (March 2020 precedent: gold -12% before +40%)
- **Dollar strength** — tariffs perceived as reducing trade deficit or attracting capital → DXY up → gold down
- **Real rate repricing** — inflation expectations vs nominal rates shifting
- **Positioning unwind** — likely record spec longs, narrow exits on reversal

### What Recovery Needs (Macro)
1. **Fed pivot signal** — dovish shift (rate cuts, QE hints) is the strongest gold catalyst
2. **Dollar weakness** — DXY roll-over, tariff softening, trade deal signals
3. **Risk asset stabilization** — eases forced-liquidation selling pressure
4. **Continued central bank buying** — structural floor if EM CBs keep buying physical at $380-400
