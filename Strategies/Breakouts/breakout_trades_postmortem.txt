HOLISTIC POST-MORTEM OF BREAKOUT OPTION TRADES

1. HIGH-LEVEL PATTERN RECOGNITION (GLOBAL VIEW)

Your PnL dispersion is asymmetric in your favor:
- Winners typically range from +500 to +1,700 USD
- Losers cluster around -250 to -350 USD, with few structural outliers

Key conclusion:
Directional skill is strong. The main driver of underperformance is option structure (volatility, timing, convexity), not market reading.

This is primarily a volatility and path-dependency problem, not a directional one.


2. MACRO / SECTOR LAYER

WHAT WORKED
Winning trades are concentrated in environments with:
- Clear macro tailwinds (Gold, Energy, Mega-cap Tech)
- Sector leadership and trend persistence
- No contradiction between macro regime and trade direction

These trades aligned with risk regime, rates, USD, and sentiment.
They benefited from volatility expansion rather than fighting it.

WHAT FAILED
Losing trades often occurred when:
- Macro narratives were crowded (USO / geopolitical risk)
- Regime uncertainty dominated (rates, bonds)
- Volatility was being sold rather than bought (index trades)

Key insight:
Breakout options fail most often when markets compress volatility, even if direction is broadly correct.


3. IDIOSYNCRATIC / SINGLE-NAME STRUCTURE

WINNING TRADES
- Clear price discovery (new highs/lows)
- Narrative aligned with price action
- Active management (partials, adaptive exits)
- Discipline when asymmetry disappeared

LOSING TRADES
- Late entries after consolidation
- Mean-reversion environments
- Trades held on thesis rather than market feedback

Many losing trades were logically correct but structurally wrong.


4. TECHNICAL STRUCTURE

WINNERS
- Entry near volatility expansion
- Break beyond Fibonacci zones
- Price aligned with key moving averages
- Predefined technical exits

LOSERS
- Entries inside Fibonacci ranges
- Price hovering around 25/50 DMA
- Lack of momentum follow-through

Key rule:
Options lose money fastest in markets that hesitate.


5. OPTION PRICING & GREEKS

WHY WINNERS PAID
- IV not elevated at entry
- Delta expansion aligned with spot
- Gamma acceleration offset theta
- Exits often preceded IV collapse

WHY LOSERS DID NOT
In several losing trades, the option was objectively too expensive even though spot did not collapse.

Failure modes:
1. IV crush
2. Theta decay during consolidation
3. Low-delta options with insufficient time
4. Repositioning strikes without extending duration

Core principle:
Options must be cheap relative to expected realized volatility, not cheap in absolute dollars.


6. CONSISTENT EDGES IN BEST TRADES

- Market-based exit logic (Fib targets, BS estimates)
- Respect for asymmetry exhaustion
- Highly liquid underlyings enabling execution edge


7. DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS FOR BREAKOUT OPTION TRADES

MACRO / REGIME
- Is the asset aligned with the dominant macro regime?
- Is volatility being bid or offered?
- Is the narrative already consensus?

STRUCTURAL PRICE CONTEXT
- Is price breaking out of value or balance?
- Continuation or first breakout attempt?
- Where is price vs 25/50/200 DMA?

VOLATILITY & OPTION PRICING
- IV percentile (30–60 day)?
- Am I paying for volatility expansion that already occurred?
- What realized volatility is required?
- How much PnL comes from delta vs vega vs gamma?

TIME & PATH DEPENDENCY
- Does slow movement still pay?
- How many flat days can the trade survive?
- Am I trading time or direction?

ASYMMETRY & EXITS
- Where does asymmetry disappear?
- What invalidates the trade before max loss?
- Is exit logic market-based or PnL-based?

BEHAVIORAL
- Would I enter this trade again today?
- Am I holding due to thesis or sunk cost?
- Is this still the same trade or a new one?
